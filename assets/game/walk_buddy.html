Your heart benefits from regular movement. Not a specific speed, not a perfect route, and not an all-or-nothing workout. Just steady activity that gets your blood moving.

Why movement helps

When your body moves, your heart rate rises gently. Your blood vessels expand to deliver oxygen to your muscles.

Practiced regularly, that expansion helps vessels stay flexible. Flexible vessels mean lower resistance. Lower resistance means lower pressure.

The goal is consistency. Walk, roll, pace, or move in another safe way that fits today.
